Ancient Times (Before 1800):

  1. Early uses: Windmills for grinding grain and water pumps, waterwheels for powering mills, geothermal energy for heating in Roman times.

    These early applications of renewable energy were innovative but had limited scope and impact on overall energy use.

1800s - Early 1900s:

  1. 1800: Alessandro Volta creates the first battery

    Paving the way for stored energy.

  2. 1836: First hydrogen fuel cell developed

    Introducing a new way to generate clean energy.

  3. 1876: William Siemens invents the first practical solar cell

    A significant step forward in harnessing solar energy.

  4. 1880s: Wind turbines see increased use in Europe and the US for electricity generation

    Wind energy begins to show its potential for large-scale electricity production.

  5. 1883: First hydroelectric power plant built in Niagara Falls

    Marking a monumental achievement in renewable energy.

  6. 1891: Nikola Tesla develops the Tesla coil

    Crucial for the advancement of AC transmission.

  7. 1908: Henry Ford introduces the Model T

    A mass-produced car that increased demand for fossil fuels.

Mid-20th Century:

  1. 1958: First solar panels used to power satellites

    Expanding the use of solar technology beyond Earth.

  2. 1973: Oil crisis sparks interest in alternative energy sources

    A pivotal moment that accelerated the search for and development of renewable energy technologies.

  3. 1979: Three Mile Island nuclear accident

    Raises significant concerns about the safety of nuclear power.

  4. 1980s: Development and production of wind turbines accelerates

    Marking a period of rapid growth in wind energy technology.

Late 20th Century - Early 21st Century:

  1. 1990s: Solar panels become more affordable and efficient

    A crucial development making solar power more accessible to the masses.

  2. 2001: Kyoto Protocol sets international greenhouse gas reduction targets

    Boosting investment and interest in renewable energy sources.

  3. 2008: Financial crisis disrupts traditional energy markets

    Further supporting the transition to renewable energy sources.

  4. 2010s: Rapid cost reductions in solar and wind power

    Making them competitive with fossil fuels for the first time.

21st Century and Beyond:

  1. 2015: Paris Agreement sets ambitious global goals for decarbonization

    A landmark agreement aiming for a sustainable low-carbon future.

  2. 2019: Renewable energy surpasses coal in global power generation for the first time

    A significant milestone in the transition to cleaner energy sources.

  3. 2020s: Focus on grid integration, energy storage, and new technologies like geothermal and biomass

    Advancing the reliability and versatility of renewable energy sources.

  4. Future: Continued growth of renewables expected, aiming for net-zero emissions by 2050 and beyond

    The global energy landscape is evolving towards a more sustainable and renewable future.
