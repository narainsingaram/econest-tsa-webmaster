20%

Renewable Energy Adoption in Homes

According to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), approximately 20% of U.S. homeowners have integrated at least one form of renewable energy solution, such as solar panels or geothermal heating, into their homes as of 2023. This represents a significant increase from just 12% in 2015, indicating a growing trend towards sustainable living.

$1,400

Cost Savings from Renewable Energy

A study by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) found that homeowners who install solar panels can save an average of $1,400 per year on their electricity bills. Over a 20-year period, these savings can accumulate to around $28,000, making renewable energy not only environmentally friendly but also economically beneficial in the long run.

30%

Tax Incentives for Green Energy

The federal government offers substantial tax credits for green energy installations. For instance, the Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows homeowners to deduct 30% of the cost of installing a solar energy system from their federal taxes. This incentive has been a crucial factor in driving the adoption of solar energy, with the Solar Energy Industries Association (SEIA) reporting that over 2 million solar installations have been completed
